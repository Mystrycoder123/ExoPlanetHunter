Goals of NASA’s Exoplanet Exploration Program

NASA’s Exoplanet Exploration Program aims to discover and characterize exoplanets (planets outside our solar system) with the following goals:

  • Identify the locations and characteristics of exoplanets around stars.
  • Determine if exoplanets can support life by assessing their atmospheres and environmental conditions.
  • Advance our understanding of planetary systems and how they form and evolve.
  • Investigate the potential for resources that could support human exploration in the future.

Exoplanets

  • 51 Pegasi b

    • 51 Pegasi b, discovered in 1995, was the first exoplanet found orbiting a sun-like star. It is a gas giant with a temperature around 1000°C, located approximately 50 light-years away in the constellation Pegasus.

  • HD 209458 b

    • HD 209458 b is a well-studied exoplanet that orbits a star similar to our sun. It is known for its unique characteristics and is a key target for studying atmospheres of exoplanets.
